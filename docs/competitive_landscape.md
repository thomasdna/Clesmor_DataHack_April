# Competitive landscape (1-page pitch slide)

## Where similar products exist

### 1) Retail site selection / location intelligence
**What they do well**
- Trade-area mapping, void analysis, competitor mapping
- Demographic overlays, scenario planning
- Portfolio planning (multi-site rollouts)

**Typical limits**
- Expensive, enterprise-oriented workflows
- Often depends on proprietary datasets (leases, spend, mobility)
- Can be a “black box” to non-analyst users

### 2) Foot-traffic / mobility analytics
**What they do well**
- Visitation, dwell time, origin-destination, time-of-day patterns
- Catchment definition and performance benchmarking

**Typical limits**
- Relies on paid mobile location panels / proprietary data
- Hard to explain and audit for a hackathon/judging context

### 3) Local SEO / presence intelligence
**What they do well**
- Category density, competitive presence, review/rating signals
- Good for “where should I market?” decisions

**Typical limits**
- Optimizes for visibility/search, not end-to-end expansion feasibility

### 4) Commercial real-estate analytics
**What they do well**
- Vacancy, rents, listings, tenant mix, development pipeline

**Typical limits**
- Data is rarely open; access is paid and fragmented
- More about “site availability” than ecosystem/market-fit triangulation

## How Expansion Copilot is different (and why it matters)

**Expansion Copilot = a lightweight site-selection workflow built on open datasets + explainable scoring**

- **Open-data-first**: Foursquare OS Places + NSW transport + ABS SEIFA (no proprietary panels required)
- **Explainable**: transparent feature formulas + score breakdown + “Why here?” drivers
- **Business-type templates**: gym / café / clinic / coworking + **Custom (beta)** taxonomy
- **Fast demo-ready UX**: shortlist → why → compare → market-fit overlay → exports

**Positioning statement**
> “Like a lightweight site-selection tool, but open-data-first and explainable—built to reduce early location mistakes.”
