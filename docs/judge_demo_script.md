# Expansion Copilot — Judge Demo Script (2 minutes)

## 2-minute click path

1. Start the app (`Opportunity map` tab loads by default).
2. Point to the tagline at the top.
3. In the sidebar:
   - Toggle **Strategy mode** between **balanced growth** → **low competition** → **ecosystem-first**.
   - Mention guardrails (min data quality, min active POIs).
4. On the map:
   - Hover a few points to show the tooltip with drivers.
5. On the right:
   - In **Top candidates**, select a top area.
   - Click **Pin selected** twice to pin 2–3 areas.
6. Click the `Why this location?` tab:
   - Read the 1–2 sentence explanation.
   - Call out “pros / risks / validate next”.
7. Click `Compare` tab:
   - Show the side-by-side view.
   - End with the closing line.

## Exact lines to say (feel free to paraphrase)

**Opening (10s)**  
“This is Expansion Copilot. It turns place data into smarter expansion decisions for a Sydney gym-chain expansion manager.”

**What it answers (10s)**  
“The question is simple: where should we open next in Sydney, and why?”

**How it works (20s)**  
“We score each area using explainable signals: supportive complements, commercial activity proxy, category diversity, and data coverage—then we penalize direct competition and saturation risk.”

**Strategy modes (20s)**  
“For the demo, I can switch strategy instantly. Low-competition prioritizes white-space; ecosystem-first prioritizes a strong wellness environment.”

**Why here (30s)**  
“When I pick an area, the app explains it in product language and shows the pros, risks, and what we’d validate in the real world—like foot traffic, site constraints, and differentiation versus nearby competitors.”

**Compare (20s)**  
“And we can compare 2–3 candidate areas side-by-side to make a clear next-step recommendation.”

**Closing (10s)**  
“Businesses do not need more map data. They need fewer bad location decisions.”

## Fallback story (if the map is slow)

“If the map feels heavy, the decision still works through the ranked Top Candidates table and Compare mode. The product is designed so the explanation and comparison flow stays fast even without interactive mapping.”
