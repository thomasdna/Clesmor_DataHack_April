# Expansion Copilot — Product Brief

## User
**Franchise expansion manager / growth lead** for a boutique or mid‑market gym chain operating in Sydney.

## Pain point
Expansion decisions are often made with “gut feel” and scattered evidence:
- Competitor research is manual and incomplete
- Teams argue about “good areas” without consistent definitions
- It’s hard to explain *why* a location is recommended (beyond “it feels busy”)

## Decision being improved
**Choose the next Sydney area to open a gym** by quickly shortlisting and comparing candidate areas with an explainable rationale.

## Why location data is the right wedge
Gym success depends heavily on **local context**:
- Nearby **competitors** (direct substitutes)
- Nearby **complements** that drive repeat footfall (grocery, transit, offices, schools)
- Density of amenities / mixed‑use intensity as a proxy for activity

Foursquare POIs are a strong starting point because they are **broad, structured, and mappable**.

## Why Sydney is the right scope
- Big enough to show meaningful geographic variation
- Bounded enough to compute quickly and demo reliably in 48 hours
- Judges can intuitively evaluate outputs (CBD vs inner west vs beaches vs north shore)

## Why gyms are the right demo category
- Easy to understand “competitors vs complements”
- Clear consumer behavior patterns (commute, errands, routine)
- Many comparable POIs exist in the dataset (gyms, studios, fitness centers)

## What success looks like in the demo
In a live demo, a user can:
1. Select **Sydney + gym category**
2. Get **top 10 candidate areas** with scores and explanations
3. Click a candidate area to see:
   - competitor count and examples
   - complement mix
   - data quality / coverage note
4. Compare 2–3 areas and export results

The outcome is a defensible answer: **“Open in Area X because it has high complement activity, manageable competition, and strong POI coverage.”**
